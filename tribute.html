MY TRIBUTE TO Dr. APJ ABDUL KALAM

"Missile Man of India"

    INFORMATION


  • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, whose full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an esteemed Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

  • Scientist and Engineer:

    A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a prominent figure in India's space and missile development programs. He played a significant role in the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was also associated with India's missile development efforts, particularly the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • Presidential Tenure:

    In 2002, he was elected as the President of India and served in this esteemed position until 2007. During his presidency, he was widely admired for his simplicity, vision for a developed India, and his interactions with students, which earned him the nickname "People's President."

  • Author and Visionary:

    Dr. Kalam was a respected author and speaker. He wrote several inspirational and motivational books, sharing his vision for a better India and motivating the youth to achieve their dreams.

  • Inspirational Figure:
    Known for his humble and unassuming nature, A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an inspirational figure, especially among the youth. He often emphasized the importance of education, hard work, and the pursuit of one's dreams.
